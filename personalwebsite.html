03 · AI Skills

Using AI the right way

From my summer AI camp, I learned how to use AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini properly — not just typing a quick question, but giving them the right information so they actually help. Here's what that covers:

Giving good context

Briefing the AI properly

Instead of a one-line question, I share the details, files, or goals the AI actually needs — the same way you'd brief a helpful classmate.

Asking it to think harder

Using "thinking mode" for hard questions

For tricky problems, I ask the AI to slow down and reason carefully instead of rushing to the first answer.

Not just trusting the first answer

Checking & improving answers

AI sometimes just agrees with you to sound nice. I know how to ask for honest, balanced feedback instead of empty praise.

Going beyond text

Images, files, and small apps

I know how to have AI read images and documents, help build small web apps and games, and use it safely and responsibly.

Based on what I studied at The AI Agent Factory's AI Prompting Crash Course. I'm happy to walk a friend, classmate, or teacher through any of these ideas — just ask!
